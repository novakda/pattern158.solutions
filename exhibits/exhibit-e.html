INVESTIGATION REPORT

EXHIBIT E — PNC AMG Journey: Client Showcases Work as Internal Model

Team Recognition · Instructional Design · February 2016
Section 1

Background

The AMG Journey was a year-long instructional design project for PNC Financial Services. The team adapted to changing client requirements throughout the engagement while maintaining creative quality. This was a large-scale, cross-functional effort involving designers, editors, developers, and project managers.

PNC ultimately elevated this project internally as a model for how to approach training design partnerships — a distinction that reflects not just the quality of the deliverables but the collaborative process that produced them.

Section 3

Findings

The AMG Journey project succeeded despite changing requirements over a year-long timeline. The client specifically identified three factors in their satisfaction:

  • Creativity — The design approach was novel enough for PNC to present internally
  • Adaptability — The team adjusted seamlessly to evolving client needs
  • Partnership — PNC called out the collaborative relationship, not just the deliverables

The fact that PNC was asked to present this series as a model design approach within their organization indicates the project exceeded standard client expectations and was recognized at institutional level.

Section 5

Client Impact

PNC Financial Services took the following actions based on their satisfaction with the AMG Journey project:

  • Presented the series internally as a model design approach for future training partnerships
  • Complimented the team on creativity, professionalism, and quality on numerous occasions
  • Called out the partnership as the primary reason for success during the project debrief
  • Specifically noted the team's ability to work toward their needs regardless of circumstances
Section 6

Significance

When a client elevates vendor work to the level of an internal model, it represents the highest form of implicit endorsement. PNC was not simply satisfied — they considered the collaboration process and output worthy of institutional replication. This reflects on the entire team's ability to maintain standards under evolving requirements over an extended engagement.

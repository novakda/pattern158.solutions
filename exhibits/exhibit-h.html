EXHIBIT H: METAL ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING

Cross-Functional Rapid Diagnosis — Technical Lead Discovery

Classification: Corroborated / Cross-Functional Resolution
Client: GP Strategies Client
Location: United States
Period: October 2019
Investigator: Dan Novak, Computer Programmer, GP Strategies
Field Report · Exhibit H · GP Strategies Client
1. Background

In October 2019, a client-facing online course on "Design for Metal Additive Manufacturing" had functionality issues — specifically, it was failing to record course completions in the LMS. Multiple stakeholders were involved and urgency was high.

The course was live and learners were completing content without receiving credit, creating both a compliance tracking issue and a learner experience problem that required immediate attention.

2. Personnel

The GP Strategies team included an LMS Configuration Specialist who identified the root cause, a Client Liaison managing customer relationships, and Dan Novak providing technical troubleshooting. Additional GP Strategies team members contributed to the engagement across various roles.

4. Root Cause Analysis

The LMS Configuration Specialist identified an LMS configuration issue causing the completion tracking failure. After adjustment, testing confirmed successful completion recording. The rapid resolution relieved significant stakeholder anxiety.

The issue was isolated to the LMS configuration layer rather than the courseware itself, which meant the fix could be applied without republishing the course content — a critical distinction under time pressure.

5. Findings

Significance

Cross-functional collaboration to quickly resolve a client-impacting issue. Three independent expressions of relief and gratitude indicate the pressure the team was under and the quality of the resolution. The word "relief" from one team member is particularly telling — it reveals the weight of the situation before the fix was found.

The directive to "share this resolution with any other appropriate parties" and "close the loop" indicates this was not a minor issue but one with visibility across multiple stakeholder groups.

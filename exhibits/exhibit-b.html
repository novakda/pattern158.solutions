INVESTIGATION REPORT

EXHIBIT B — Leadership Recognition Chain (Electric Boat Engagement)

Multi-Source Corroboration · August 2018
Section 1

Background

This exhibit documents the multi-level recognition cascade following Dan Novak's on-site technical engagement at General Dynamics Electric Boat in August 2018.

After a 2-day on-site visit, recognition came from three independent channels before the formal trip report was even submitted. This exhibit traces how client satisfaction propagated through GP Strategies' leadership hierarchy, demonstrating genuine rather than perfunctory appreciation.

Section 3

Findings

Recognition of the Electric Boat on-site engagement propagated through GP Strategies' leadership hierarchy via multiple independent channels. The timeline below establishes that praise was not coordinated or solicited — it arose organically from multiple touchpoints with the client.

Section 4

Evidence Chain

  1. August 17, 2018 — Charlie Rando (EB client, Chief of Learning Services) emails his internal EB leadership chain, summarizing the visit outcomes and expressing satisfaction with the engagement.
  2. August 20, 2018 — Kimberly Vahlsing (Sr. Director) hears from her EB contacts and emails Dan directly, without prompting from Dan's management chain.
  3. August 21, 2018 — Thomas Pizer (Director) hears from three independent sources — Chris Sproule (SVP), Tracey Nicholson, and Quinn Gladu — and emails Dan to relay the feedback.
Section 5

Primary Source Testimony

I hear you did an AWESOME job onsite! EB really appreciated your support. Thank you for making the trip and doing all the great things you do!
Kimberly Vahlsing — Sr. Director, Learning Solutions, GP Strategies (August 20, 2018)
Thanks for all of the great work you did out there. I heard about your work from several folks including Chris Sproule, Tracey Nicholson, and Quinn Gladu. Thanks for your hard work!
Thomas Pizer — Director of Learning Technologies, GP Strategies (August 21, 2018)
Section 6

Significance

Recognition escalated from client technical lead (Charlie Rando at Electric Boat) to GP Strategies Sr. Director and Director levels, with at least three independent corroborating sources. This pattern indicates genuine rather than perfunctory praise:

  • The Sr. Director reached out before the formal trip report was filed
  • The Director cited three named sources who independently contacted him
  • The sources spanned multiple levels of the organization (SVP, team members)
  • Recognition came within 4 days of the on-site visit concluding

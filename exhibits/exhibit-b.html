EXHIBIT B: MULTI-LEVEL RECOGNITION

Two Independent Cascades Through GP Strategies Leadership

Classification: Pattern Analysis
Client: General Dynamics Electric Boat (via GP Strategies)
Period: 2018 – 2019
Investigator: Dan Novak, Computer Programmer, GP Strategies
Field Report · Exhibit B · Electric Boat (via GP Strategies)
1. Background

This exhibit documents two independent recognition cascades that followed Dan Novak's on-site technical engagements at General Dynamics Electric Boat — the first in August 2018, the second in April 2019.

In both cases, recognition propagated through GP Strategies' leadership hierarchy via multiple independent channels, unsolicited, before formal trip reports were filed. The fact that the same pattern repeated eight months apart — from a different set of triggers, through different intermediaries — indicates genuine impact rather than procedural courtesy.

For the technical details of the Electric Boat engagement, see Exhibit A.

2. Personnel

Dan Novak served as Technical Consultant for GP Strategies and was the subject of both recognition cascades following his on-site work at Electric Boat in August 2018 and April 2019.

The GP Strategies management chain included the Director of Learning Technologies, who relayed feedback from multiple independent sources after both visits; the Sr. Director of Learning Solutions, who served as executive sponsor authorizing the on-site trips and brought 15+ years of GP Strategies leadership; and the Senior Vice President of Learning Solutions, who initiated the EB engagement in September 2017 and independently praised Dan's work.

On the Electric Boat side, the Chief of Learning Services served as primary client contact and authored internal EB leadership summaries after both visits. The GP Strategies on-site EB team (3 members) independently relayed positive feedback to the Director following the April 2019 visit.

4. First Recognition Cascade — August 2018

Following a 2-day on-site visit to resolve cross-domain SCORM communication failures, recognition propagated through GP Strategies' hierarchy before the formal trip report was filed.

  1. August 17, 2018 — The EB client contact (Chief of Learning Services) emails his internal EB leadership chain, summarizing visit outcomes.
  2. August 21, 2018 — The Director of Learning Technologies hears from multiple sources and emails Dan directly to relay the feedback.
  3. Same week — The Sr. Director reaches out independently, without prompting from Dan's management chain.
I hear you did an AWESOME job onsite! EB really appreciated your support. Thank you for making the trip and doing all the great things you do!
Director of Learning Technologies, GP Strategies — (August 21, 2018)
5. Second Recognition Cascade — April 2019

Eight months later, a 12-day on-site engagement produced the same pattern — recognition propagating through the hierarchy via independent channels, with named corroborating sources.

  1. April 10, 2019 — The EB Chief of Learning Services emails GP leadership praising Dan's technical expertise and its direct impact on Flash conversion timelines.
  2. April 19, 2019 — A senior GP team member responds, affirming the decision to send Dan and his colleague as "the A team."
  3. May 8, 2019 — The Director of Learning Technologies forwards the recognition thread, citing three named sources — Chris Sproule, Tracey Nicholson, and Quinn Gladu — who independently relayed positive feedback.
Thanks for all of the great work you did out there. I heard about your work from several folks including Chris Sproule, Tracey Nicholson, and Quinn Gladu. Thanks for your hard work!
Director of Learning Technologies, GP Strategies — (May 8, 2019)
6. Pattern Analysis

Both cascades share structural characteristics that distinguish genuine impact from procedural courtesy:

  • Speed — Recognition emerged within days of each visit, not weeks
  • Independence — Multiple channels activated without coordination or solicitation
  • Cross-level propagation — Feedback traveled from client (EB) through senior leadership (SVP, Sr. Director) to direct management (Director)
  • Specificity — Named sources, named outcomes, and specific technical contributions cited
  • Repeatability — The same pattern emerged from two different engagements, eight months apart
7. Significance

A single recognition event can be attributed to politics, timing, or courtesy. Two independent cascades — through different intermediaries, triggered by different technical outcomes, eight months apart — constitute a pattern. The Director of Learning Technologies cited independent sources in both instances, confirming that recognition was not solicited or coordinated.

The technical work that triggered both recognition cascades involved:

Platforms: Cornerstone OnDemand LMS, Cornerstone Network Player, Articulate Storyline, Adobe Flash/Captivate

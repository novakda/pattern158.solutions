EXHIBIT C: XYLEME AUTOMATION

Reverse-Engineering the Publishing API to Save 600+ Hours

Classification: Process Engineering / API Integration
Client: GP Strategies (Internal)
Period: December 2015
Investigator: Dan Novak, Computer Programmer, GP Strategies
Field Report · Exhibit C · GP Strategies (Internal)
1. Background

In 2015, GP Strategies undertook a massive content refresh of their enterprise course library. A total of 1,216 lessons needed to be verified, fixed, and republished through the Xyleme content authoring platform. The project involved 15+ team members over several months and represented one of the largest single content operations in the team's history.

2. The Problem

Publishing lessons from Xyleme was a manual, time-consuming process. Each lesson had to be individually published through the Xyleme interface — selecting the lesson, configuring output parameters, initiating the publish, and waiting for confirmation. With 1,216 lessons needing multiple publish cycles, the labor cost was enormous.

The team collectively needed to verify that all content in every lesson matched what was currently being served to customers. This meant each lesson went through multiple rounds of verification and republishing, compounding the manual effort exponentially.

3. The Solution

Dan Novak used the Fiddler network debugging tool to analyze and integrate with Xyleme's publishing API. By capturing and analyzing the HTTP traffic between the browser and Xyleme's servers, he identified the exact sequence of API requests needed to trigger a publish operation.

He then built an automation tool that could batch-publish lessons unattended — eliminating the need for a human to manually click through each lesson's publish workflow. This saved an estimated 600+ hours of manual labor across the project.

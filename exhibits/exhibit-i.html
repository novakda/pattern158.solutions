INVESTIGATION REPORT

EXHIBIT I — TD MIC Project: Accessibility & Code Review

Corroborated · Accessibility Compliance · August 2022
Section 1

Background

TD Bank (Toronto-Dominion Bank) engaged GP Strategies for MIC (Mandatory and Important Courses) development. Dan Novak contributed software engineering and accessibility review work. The project involved code of conduct training, accessibility compliance, and cross-functional collaboration between GP Strategies teams in North America.

The project required coordination between Canadian and US-based team members, spanning multiple disciplines including instructional design, media production, software engineering, and WCAG accessibility compliance verification.

Section 4

Accessibility Scope

Dan performed accessibility testing and review as part of the TD MIC project, including:

  • Keyboard navigation testing across all interactive elements
  • Screen reader compatibility verification
  • WCAG compliance verification against established standards
  • Research into tools including WebAIM, The A11Y Project checklists, and screen reader testing methodologies

This work was performed alongside core software engineering responsibilities, reflecting a dual-track contribution to the project.

Section 5

Findings

Significance

Demonstrates breadth beyond core SCORM/LMS expertise into accessibility compliance — an increasingly critical competency in enterprise learning technology. WCAG compliance is no longer optional for financial institutions; it is a legal and regulatory requirement.

The cross-border coordination between Canadian and US-based team members adds logistical complexity that the team navigated successfully. The project's scope — mandatory and important courses — means this work directly impacts TD Bank's regulatory compliance posture.

EXHIBIT I: TD BANK ACCESSIBILITY

Enterprise Accessibility Methodology Across 4 Major Banking Clients

Corroborated · WCAG 2.1 · Enterprise Initiative · Accessibility · 2022-2024
1. Background

Dan Novak's accessibility practice evolved organically through client engagements rather than a top-down directive. The practice originated with HSBC's Simple Learning courses, where accessibility requirements first surfaced in the context of compliance eLearning. As GP Strategies' banking portfolio expanded, the methodology matured through accessibility testing for PNC Bank and TD Bank Articulate Storyline courses — interactive content that demanded testing protocols beyond anything standard resources provided.

The TD Bank engagement (2022-2024) represented the practice at full maturity. TD MIC (Mutual Investment Counsellor) training courses required WCAG 2.1 and Section 508 compliance testing for Articulate Storyline content. Dan executed the entire accessibility evaluation independently, applying the methodology refined across prior banking engagements to deliver confirmation statements, issue triage, and remediation recommendations.

The initiative peaked in 2022 with 479 accessibility-related emails out of 4,173 total professional correspondence — reflecting the scale of the practice Dan built across multiple banking clients.

2. Personnel

Dan Novak served as Accessibility Evaluator and Enterprise Methodology Lead, handling the entire TD Bank accessibility evaluation independently. He applied methodology refined through prior HSBC Simple Learning and PNC Bank Storyline accessibility engagements. The same approach was also deployed for UOB compliance courses.

The Senior Learning Project Manager at GP Strategies coordinated the TD MIC project and provided program-level oversight.

3. The Standards Gap

The core challenge Dan solved: "How do you test something with no standard?"

Interactive eLearning content — particularly Articulate Storyline courses with custom navigation, gamification, and dynamic state management — exhibited non-deterministic screen reader behavior. Unlike static web content, where WCAG guidelines provide clear success criteria, interactive courseware lacked established testing protocols.

Existing accessibility resources (WebAIM, The A11Y Project) provided foundational knowledge for static HTML but offered no guidance for testing screen reader interactions with authoring tool output, branching scenarios, or custom JavaScript-driven navigation patterns. The industry gap was not in standards themselves (WCAG 2.1 / Section 508) but in applying those standards to complex interactive content.

The gap became apparent through successive banking engagements — starting with HSBC Simple Learning courses, then PNC Bank and TD Bank Storyline content — where each new client's interactive courseware exposed limitations in existing testing approaches. What began as ad hoc accessibility review evolved into a systematic enterprise-level practice.

4. Methodology

Dan developed the accessibility testing practice through iterative refinement across successive banking engagements, starting with HSBC and maturing through PNC Bank and TD Bank.

Research Phase

  • Self-directed study of WCAG 2.1 specification and Section 508 requirements
  • Championed ARIA Authoring Practices as the bridge between WCAG principles and interactive content
  • Learned JAWS and NVDA screen readers to understand assistive technology user experience
  • Analyzed Articulate Storyline HTML output to map authoring tool patterns to WCAG success criteria

Template Creation

  • Designed accessibility confirmation statement templates for client reporting
  • Established issue triage categories (critical / major / minor) aligned with WCAG conformance levels
  • Created remediation recommendation documentation that translated technical WCAG violations into actionable instructional design guidance

Testing Protocol

  • Keyboard navigation testing across all interactive elements (tab order, focus management, keyboard traps)
  • Screen reader compatibility verification (JAWS and NVDA) with focus on announcement clarity and navigation logic
  • Cross-browser testing to identify browser-specific ARIA implementation inconsistencies
  • Sprint-based workflow integration (SCRUM: TD MIC meetings) to align accessibility verification with development cycles

The methodology was refined through deployment across four major banking clients: HSBC (origin), PNC Bank, TD Bank, and UOB. The templates and protocols Dan created became the standard for GP Strategies' accessibility practice.

5. Accessibility Scope (TD MIC)

Dan performed comprehensive accessibility testing for TD Bank's Mutual Investment Counsellor (TD MIC) training program, delivered as Articulate Storyline courses. The engagement included:

Testing Deliverables

  • WCAG 2.1 compliance verification against Level A and AA success criteria
  • Section 508 conformance testing for U.S. federal accessibility requirements
  • Screen reader testing (JAWS and NVDA) with detailed interaction documentation
  • Keyboard navigation testing across all course modules and assessments

Reporting & Remediation

  • Accessibility confirmation statements for client delivery
  • Issue triage with severity classification and WCAG reference mapping
  • Remediation recommendations with specific Articulate Storyline implementation guidance
  • Cross-functional collaboration with instructional designers and media teams to resolve accessibility barriers

The TD Bank engagement was executed solo — Dan handled the entire accessibility evaluation independently, applying the methodology refined through prior HSBC and PNC Bank engagements.

7. Findings

Significance

Dan did not simply test for accessibility compliance — he built the testing practice through successive client engagements. The challenge was methodological: creating a repeatable, scalable protocol for evaluating non-deterministic screen reader behavior in interactive eLearning where no industry standards existed.

The initiative demonstrates strategic capability beyond tactical execution. What started with HSBC Simple Learning courses evolved into a methodology deployed across four major banking clients. The 479 accessibility emails in 2022 reflect sustained execution, not a one-time deliverable.

For financial institutions, WCAG compliance is not optional — it is a legal and regulatory requirement. Dan's methodology directly impacted TD Bank's regulatory compliance posture and provided GP Strategies with a competitive differentiator in the enterprise learning market.
